Earth is the third planet from the sun and the only astronomical object to harbor life. According to radiometric dating and other sources of evidence, Earth formed over 4.5 billion years ago. Earth's gravity interacts with other objects in space, especially the Sun and Moon, which is Earth's only natural satellite. Earth orbits the sun in 365.256 days, a period known as an Earth sidereal year. During this time, Earth rotates around it's axis 266.256 times. Earth's axis of rotation is tilted with respect to its orbital plane, producing seasons on Earth. The gravitational interaction between Earth and the Moon causes tides, stabilizes Earth's orientation on its axis, and gradually slows it's rotation. Earth is the densest planet in the Solar System and the largest and most massive of the four rocky planets.
