The pineapple is a tropical plant with an edible fruit, also called a pineapple, and the most economically significant plant in the family Bromeliaceae. The pineapple is indegenous to South America where it has been cultivated for many centuries.

display: inline-block;

The introduction of the pineapple cultivation to Europe in the 17C made it a significant cultural icon of luxury. Since the 1820s pineapple has been comercially grown in greenhouses and many tropical plantations and it is the third most important tropical fruit in the world production.

display: block;

In 20C, Hawaii was a dominant producer of pineapples, especially for the US, but in 2016, Costa Rica, Brazil, and the Philippines accounted for nearly one-third of the world's production of pineapples.
